## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 What This Document Is

This is the product requirements document for Lexos — a shared internal vocabulary database that Cosmoplex's voice AI products use to understand how people actually speak. Lexos is a platform product, not a consumer product. Its direct customers are internal product teams and the voice AI systems they build. End consumers never interact with Lexos directly.

### 1.2 What Lexos Is

Lexos is middleware. It sits between a voice workflow and the ASR and LLM engines that the workflow already uses. It does not own the ASR connection, does not stream audio, and does not build a custom speech model. Its job is to make any ASR engine hear better and any LLM understand better — by preloading the right vocabulary context before a session starts, and resolving raw transcripts to clean intents in real time between STT output and LLM input.

Unlike a generic dictionary lookup, Lexos preloads targeted library subsets based on the specific workflow, language profile, and user context. South Indian voice ordering preloads South Indian languages plus QSR vocabulary. Bus ticket ordering preloads route and payment vocabulary. Personal mobile usage builds a persistent vocabulary profile for the individual user, following them across all voice-enabled apps. The right vocabulary is in memory before the user speaks — not fetched per utterance.

### 1.3 What This Document Does Not Cover

This PRD does not cover WiseOrder, AI 101, Myna, or any other Cosmoplex consumer product. Lexos is infrastructure. This document specifies what Lexos does, how it behaves, and what success looks like for Lexos itself.

## 2. Problem Statement

### 2.1 The Core Problem

4.1 billion people cannot effectively use technology products built in English. When a voice AI product tries to serve these users, it hits two compounding failures.

First, speech recognition engines are trained primarily on English and standard formal speech. When a user in Mumbai says "menu show karo", the ASR engine either mishears or transcribes it incorrectly.

Second, even when transcription is correct, LLMs are poor at interpreting colloquial and code-mixed speech without explicit context. A transcript like "bhai menu dikh" is semantically clear to a native Hindi speaker but opaque to a standard LLM system prompt.

### 2.2 Why the Status Quo Fails

| Pain point | Current state | Cost |
|-----------|--------------|------|
| No shared vocabulary | Each product team builds its own phrase lists from scratch | Duplicated work, inconsistent quality, no cross-product learning |
| No ASR biasing | Speech engines operate without domain vocabulary priming | High WER on domain-specific and code-mixed phrases |
| No intent resolution layer | LLMs receive raw colloquial transcripts | Wrong or incomplete downstream responses |
| No correction history | When a phrase mapping is wrong, the fix is an overwrite | History lost; no audit trail for quality |
| No miss signal | Failed intent matches disappear into logs | No automatic identification of vocabulary gaps |
| No eval harness | Human review is the only quality gate | Reviewers can approve incorrect entries |

### 2.3 The Opportunity

A shared vocabulary database, built once, versioned, and served across all Cosmoplex voice products, eliminates the duplicated effort, creates a compounding learning loop, and gives every product the same quality of vocabulary coverage. Every miss Lexos learns from improves every product that uses it.

## 3. Goals and Non-Goals

### 3.1 Goals

1. Serve accurate, language-specific, domain-specific vocabulary context to any Cosmoplex voice AI product through a consistent API — regardless of which ASR engine or LLM the product uses.
2. Provide three integration vectors: LLM injection, ASR biasing (priming), and SDK enrichment (real-time intent resolution).
3. Maintain a human-gated learning loop: every miss is captured, ranked, fed back into a generation pipeline, and reviewed before going live.
4. Ensure every promoted entry passes automated eval regression checks (IRAL v0) before a human can approve it.
5. Support personalisation at two levels: B2B tenant configuration and individual user profile learning.
6. Store all corrections as invalidation events with full bi-temporal validity.
7. Support 40 languages, Tier A (15 languages) at launch, Tier B/C on demand.
8. Be ASR-agnostic: normalized vocabulary translated per engine via thin adapters.
9. Be LLM-agnostic: works with GPT-4o, Claude, Gemini, Sarvam-M, Llama 3, or any model with a system prompt.

> **Current launch scope (2026-06):** 5 active languages — hi-IN, kn-IN, mr-IN,
> ta-IN, te-IN — and 11 focused QSR intents. bn-IN, gu-IN, es-ES, ar-AE are
> disabled but retained (re-enable via `scripts/toggle-languages.ts`). The wider
> 40-language / 15-Tier-A ambition above is the roadmap target, not the live set.

### 3.2 Non-Goals

- Lexos does not handle TTS.
- Lexos does not manage voice AI conversation flow, session state, or multi-turn dialogue.
- Lexos does not generate LLM responses.
- Lexos does not own the ASR streaming connection. It primes the engine; the product manages the session.
- Lexos does not build a custom ASR model. It works with existing engines via their vocabulary biasing APIs.
- Lexos does not provide a consumer-facing UI.
- Lexos does not include live, real-time self-training. The generation loop is batched and gated.
- Lexos does not manage outlet-facing vocabulary configuration. Changes go through the generation and review pipeline.

## 5. Functional Requirements

### 5.1 Personalisation Model

Lexos applies personalisation in a layered stack. Every session assembles its vocabulary library by combining four layers in order.

| Layer | What it is | How it gets populated | Example |
|-------|-----------|----------------------|---------|
| 1. Global corpus | Shared vocabulary for all products and all tenants | Generation pipeline + miss loop + semantic promote loop | LEXOS_SHOW_MENU in Hindi, Tamil, Kannada |
| 2. Workflow library | Vocabulary scoped to a specific workflow type | Configured at workflow definition, refined from usage | QSR domain intents: add item, confirm order |
| 3. Tenant profile | Vocabulary and language configuration specific to one B2B deployment. Configured at onboarding. | B2B customer seeds via onboarding API. Miss loop fills gaps. | Theobroma Cafe: menu items, Mumbai register, English-dominant |
| 4. User profile | Individual person's vocabulary, native language, geography, explicit preferences. Follows them across all products. | Learned automatically from usage. User can set explicit preferences. | A Chennai user who habitually says 'saapidum' instead of 'eat' |

> The stack is additive — each layer adds vocabulary, it does not replace the layer below. Searched in reverse order (most specific first). The first match wins.

### 5.1a Tenant Profile — B2B Configuration

| Configuration field | What it means | Theobroma example | Saravana Bhavan example |
|--------------------|--------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Primary language | Dominant language of the customer base | en-IN (English-dominant) | ta-IN (Tamil-dominant) |
| Geographic region | City or region | Mumbai, Maharashtra | Chennai, Tamil Nadu |
| Register preference | Formal, colloquial, or mixed | Mixed — casual English + Hindi | Colloquial Tamil + some English |
| Customer demographic | Age range, familiarity with tech, literacy profile | Urban 20s-30s, tech-comfortable | Mixed age, varied tech familiarity |
| Seed vocabulary | Product-specific terms not in the global corpus | 'dark indulgence', 'choco lava', 'lemon tart' | 'ghee pongal', 'rava idli', 'filter kaapi' |
| Tenant-specific intents | Intents unique to this deployment | LEXOS_ASK_EGGLESS | LEXOS_ASK_JAIN |

The same principle applies for ticket booking: Mumbai local train vs Pune intercity vs tier 2 town — same workflow, different tenant profile on top.

### 5.1b User Profile — Individual Personalisation

| Profile component | What it stores | How it is built | How it is used |
|------------------|---------------|----------------|----------------|
| Native language | The language the user is most comfortable in | Inferred from phrase patterns, confirmed by usage frequency | Weights vocabulary library toward their dominant language |
| Geographic region | City or region inferred from vocabulary and usage patterns | Inferred from place names, regional terms | Loads region-specific vocabulary variants |
| Explicit preferences | Language and register preferences the user has set directly | User-set via preferences API — optional | Overrides inferred profile for those dimensions |
| Habitual phrases | Phrases this user says frequently that resolved via semantic path | Logged from lexos_enrich_calls — semantic matches with correct downstream outcome | Promoted to explicit variants for instant exact match |
| Personal vocabulary | Terms this user introduced that weren't in the corpus | Logged from misses subsequently resolved | Added to their personal profile, available on any product |
| Cross-app continuity | Profile is product-agnostic | Stored against user_id (tenant_id = user_uuid), not product_id | A user gets the same personal vocabulary in all Lexos-integrated products |

|-----|-------------|---------|

### 5.10 Graduated Autonomy in the Staging Gate

| Stake level | Entry type | Required gate |
|-------------|-----------|--------------|
| Low | New variant of existing intent, existing language | Auto-approve if IRAL eval passes. Logged for spot-review. |
| Medium | New variant of existing intent, new language | One native-speaker reviewer required. |
| High | New intent ID, any language | Two reviewers required. IRAL regression check must pass. |
| Critical | Intent maps to a financial or safety action | Two reviewers + IRAL check + 7-day shadow period. |

### 5.11 Human Review UI

| Field | Why mandatory |
|-------|--------------|
| Intent ID + plain English description | Reviewer must understand what the intent means |
| Language + domain + tier (A/B/C) | Context for locale and domain appropriateness |
| All four variant heads side by side | Reviewing in isolation causes approval errors |

## 6. Data Schema

See `.claude/rules/schema.md` for the full SQL migration.

### 6.1 Core Tables

| Table | What it holds | Key columns |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| lexos_intents | Canonical intent IDs — the language-agnostic goals | intent_id, intent_name, domain, product_id (NULL = global) |
| lexos_terms | One row per phrase per language per domain | intent_id, language_code, domain, tier, product_id, tenant_id, active, valid_from, valid_until, superseded_by, hit_count, last_hit_at, success_after_hit, miss_after_hit |
| term_variants | The actual text — one row per variant type | term_id, variant_type (FORMAL/COLLOQUIAL/CODE_MIXED/PHONETIC), value, confidence |
| vector_embeddings | Meaning vectors for semantic search | term_id, embedding (vector), model_version, model_name |
| lexos_entries_staging | AI-generated entries awaiting review | All term fields + status, rollback_of, eval_passed, eval_regression |
| lexos_misses | Every utterance the system failed to match | utterance, lang, domain, product_id, tenant_id, frequency |
| lexos_enrich_calls | Trace log of every SDK call | input_phrase, matched_intent, match_type, latency_ms, downstream_outcome |
| audit_log | Immutable record of every human correction | term_id, field_changed, old_value, new_value, editor, timestamp |
| tenant_profiles | Configuration profile for each B2B tenant deployment | tenant_id, primary_language, geographic_region, register_preference, seed_vocabulary JSONB |
| user_profiles | Individual user personalisation profile | user_id, native_language, geographic_region, explicit_preferences JSONB, learned_patterns JSONB |

### 6.2 Day-One Schema Constraints

> **CRITICAL:** `ALTER TABLE vector_embeddings ALTER COLUMN embedding SET STORAGE PLAIN`  
> Run before inserting any vector data. Prevents TOAST from making similarity search catastrophically slow.

**Bi-temporal validity** — `valid_from, valid_until, superseded_by` on lexos_terms. Corrections are invalidation events. SDK filters: `WHERE NOW() BETWEEN valid_from AND COALESCE(valid_until, 'infinity')`

**Usage analytics** — `hit_count, last_hit_at, success_after_hit, miss_after_hit` on lexos_terms. Entries with zero hits in 90 days are sunset review candidates.

**Model versioning** — `model_version, model_name` on vector_embeddings. Required for backfill-safe embedding model upgrades.

## 8. Non-Functional Requirements

### 8.1 Latency

| Path | Target p99 | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Exact match (/enrich step 1) | ~5ms | Equality check against indexed COLLOQUIAL/CODE_MIXED variants |
| Semantic match (/enrich step 2) | ~80ms | Cloudflare Workers AI embedding + pgvector cosine similarity |
| No match (/enrich step 3) | Instant | Async log — never blocks LLM call |
| Overall SDK p99 at MVP | ~100ms | Acceptable for voice; 100ms imperceptible to the user |
| Overall SDK p99 with Redis (Phase 2) | ~10ms | Redis eliminates pgvector for known phrases |
| /retrieve/llm-context | <200ms | With Cohere rerank in Phase 2 |
| /retrieve/asr-bias | <500ms | One-time setup call per session; latency tolerant |

> **The '<40ms' claim is exact-match only.** The correct overall p99 commitment at MVP is ~100ms.

### 8.2 Scalability

| Phase | Infrastructure | Upgrade trigger |
|-------|--------------|----------------|
| Phase 1 — MVP | Postgres + pgvector, HNSW m=16 ef=128, no cache | Default — ship this |
| Phase 2 — Cache | Add Redis inverted index for SDK hot path | SDK p99 exceeds 60ms in production |
| Phase 3 — Scale | Add Qdrant as dedicated vector DB | Vector corpus exceeds 500,000 entries |

### 8.3 Reliability

- The SDK never throws an exception. Returns { intent: null, confidence: 0 } within 10ms on any failure.
- The consuming product is never blocked by a Lexos failure.
- Phase 2: SDK ships with cached snapshot of top-1,000 entries per (lang, domain) as local fallback.

### 8.4 Observability

- Every lexos.enrich() call emits a trace span (LangSmith, Braintrust, or Weave).
- lexos_enrich_calls provides queryable latency, match type distribution, downstream outcome.
- lexos_misses provides queryable miss frequency — primary signal for generation pipeline prioritisation.
- IRAL eval scores tracked per language, reported weekly. Declining score = early warning of corpus quality degradation.

### 8.5 Middleware Architecture — What Lexos Does Not Own

> - Lexos does not own the ASR streaming connection. It primes the engine; the product manages the session.
> - Lexos does not build or run a custom ASR model.
> - Lexos does not generate LLM responses.
> - Deepgram Nova-3 appears in examples because it is WiseOrder's chosen engine. It is not a Lexos dependency.

### 8.6 API Service Vision

| Capability | Internal (now) | External API (future) |

| Authentication | X-Product-ID header, internal trust | API key + JWT, rate-limited per key |

The only engineering additions needed: API gateway with authentication and rate limiting, billing metering layer, terms-of-service envelope.

### 8.7 Security and Data

- All API calls require X-Product-ID authentication. No public endpoints at MVP.
- tenant_id isolation enforced at the query layer.
- Personal vocabulary profiles (tenant_id = user_uuid) strictly isolated.
- Voice data subject to Cosmoplex data handling policy. For DPDP Act (India): verify data residency before enabling Deepgram (US-headquartered). Sarvam and BHASHINI are compliant alternatives.
- The audit_log is append-only and immutable.

## 12. Decisions Log

| Decision | Date | Rationale | What would reverse it |
|---------|------|-----------|----------------------|
| ANALOGY variant dropped; replaced by CODE_MIXED | May 2026 | ANALOGY expensive to generate, rarely useful for ASR biasing. CODE_MIXED is where voice AI actually fails in multilingual markets. | Evidence that a consuming product genuinely needs ANALOGY. Reintroduce as optional 5th variant gated by product_id. |
| Picovoice Rhino ruled out | May 2026 | Closed-domain — only pre-defined phrases, no code-mixed Hinglish support. | A future Myna deployment requiring purely offline operation. |
| Batch size locked at 15 per Claude API call | May 2026 | Batch-50 hits ~25% JSON parse failure. Batch-15 stays under 8%. | A new Claude model with significantly improved structured output reliability at larger batch sizes, verified by measurement. |
| /retrieve/asr-bias made ASR-agnostic with per-engine adapters | June 2026 | WiseOrder's ASR stack includes engines not in the Lexos original design. Locking to Deepgram format would require a rewrite. | No new ASR engines required beyond Deepgram and Whisper for 12+ months. |
| FalkorDB rejected in favour of pgvector | May 2026 | Lexos's query pattern is vector similarity, not graph traversal. pgvector eliminates a second database. | Corpus structure evolves to require complex multi-hop intent relationship queries. |
| No tenant admin UI for outlets | June 2026 | The miss loop captures outlet-specific slang automatically. | Evidence that a meaningful percentage of outlet operators actively want to manage vocabulary. |
